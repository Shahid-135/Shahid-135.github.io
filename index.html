01 Abstract

Multimodal social media data during crisis events presents significant challenges for classification due to noisy visual content, short or ambiguous text, and weak alignment between modalities. Existing multimodal approaches often rely on generic fusion strategies or task-agnostic alignment mechanisms that do not explicitly account for domain-specific semantic structure or feature reliability.

We propose CAFuNet (Context-Aligned Fusion Network), a multimodal classification architecture for humanitarian event analysis. CAFuNet introduces a dual topic-conditioning mechanism that injects corpus-induced topic representations into both visual and textual encoders, a Context-Gated Calibration (CGC) module that modulates feature magnitudes based on contextual consistency, and a Projected Bilinear Block Fusion (PBBF) head capturing low-rank multiplicative cross-modal interactions.

Experiments on CrisisMMD and TSEqD show macro-F1 gains of +4.35 and +2.56 points over strong multimodal baselines, and CAFuNet surpasses zero-shot Gemini 2.0 Flash by over +28.75 F1 points — validating that specialized domain-aware architectures outperform general-purpose foundation models in high-stakes humanitarian classification.

02 Method

CAFuNet addresses the semantic reliability gap — where one modality provides critical evidence while the other introduces noise or contradiction — through three tightly coupled modules applied before and during fusion.

🗂️
Module 01

Dual-Topic Conditioning

Topics are induced from the training corpus via BERTopic and curated by NPMI coherence. Learnable embeddings are prepended to both text tokens and visual patch sequences — grounding both encoders in the same domain-specific semantic scaffold from layer one. A Topic Alignment Projector (TAP) enables cross-modal co-adaptation.

🎛️
Module 02

Context-Gated Calibration

A learnable combination of Gaussian, Sigmoid, and Trapezoidal membership functions computes per-feature gating scores. This multiplicatively modulates features — amplifying reliable signals (G>0) and suppressing noisy ones (G<0) before fusion. CGC provides a data-driven, non-linear reliability policy crucial for noisy crisis data.

🔀
Module 03

Projected Bilinear Block Fusion

B parallel interaction blocks project each modality into a lower-dimensional space and compute element-wise products (ReLU gated). Block outputs are concatenated to form a rich fused representation capturing diverse, disentangled cross-modal interactions via efficient low-rank bilinear approximation.
