CAFuNet: Context-Aligned Topic Conditioning
and Calibrated Fusion for Multimodal Crisis Informatics

Abstract

Multimodal social media data during crisis events presents significant challenges for classification due to noisy visual content, short or ambiguous text, and weak alignment between modalities. Existing approaches rely on generic fusion strategies or task-agnostic alignment mechanisms that do not account for domain-specific semantic structure or feature reliability.

We propose CAFuNet (Context-Aligned Fusion Network), designed for humanitarian event analysis. CAFuNet introduces: (i) a dual topic-conditioning mechanism injecting corpus-induced topic representations into both visual and textual encoders, (ii) a Context-Gated Calibration (CGC) module that modulates feature magnitudes based on contextual consistency, and (iii) a Projected Bilinear Block Fusion (PBBF) head capturing low-rank multiplicative cross-modal interactions.

Experiments on CrisisMMD and TSEqD show macro-F1 gains of +4.35 and +2.56 points over strong baselines, and CAFuNet surpasses zero-shot Gemini 2.0 Flash by over +28.75 F1 points.

Method

CAFuNet addresses the semantic reliability gap — where one modality provides critical evidence while the other introduces noise or contradiction — through three synergistic modules applied before and during fusion.

01

Dual-Topic Conditioning

Topics are induced from the training corpus via BERTopic and curated by NPMI coherence. Learnable topic embeddings are prepended to both text tokens and visual patch sequences, grounding both encoders in the same domain-specific semantic scaffold from layer one.

02

Context-Gated Calibration

A learnable combination of Gaussian, Sigmoid, and Trapezoidal membership functions computes a per-feature gating score. This multiplicatively modulates feature magnitudes — amplifying reliable signals (G > 0) and suppressing noisy ones (G < 0) before fusion.

03

Projected Bilinear Block Fusion

B parallel interaction blocks project each modality into a lower-dimensional space and compute element-wise products. Block outputs are concatenated to form a rich fused representation capturing diverse, disentangled cross-modal interactions via efficient low-rank approximation.

Robustness & Efficiency

CAFuNet achieves 90.32 ± 0.20 F1 across five random seeds (vs. MMA: 87.00 ± 0.30), confirming stable, reproducible performance. Under modality degradation (blank images, Gaussian blur, text truncation, up to 20% cross-modal shuffle), CAFuNet consistently leads all baselines and degrades most gracefully — demonstrating that CGC suppresses unreliable signals before fusion rather than propagating noise. At ~150M parameters and ~22ms inference (batch 32, single GPU), CAFuNet is far more efficient than autoregressive VLMs (>200ms).
