01 CAFuNet · ACL 2025

Shahid Shafi Dar, Mihir Kanchan Karandikar, Chandravardhan Singh Raghaw, Pranjal Pandey, Nagendra Kumar

Figure 1. Overview of the CAFuNet architecture — dual-topic conditioning, Context-Gated Calibration, and Projected Bilinear Block Fusion.

Abstract

Multimodal social media data during crisis events presents significant challenges for classification due to noisy visual content, short or ambiguous text, and weak alignment between modalities. Existing multimodal approaches often rely on generic fusion strategies or task-agnostic alignment mechanisms that do not explicitly account for domain-specific semantic structure or feature reliability.

We propose CAFuNet (Context-Aligned Fusion Network), a multimodal classification architecture for humanitarian event analysis. CAFuNet introduces a dual topic-conditioning mechanism that injects corpus-induced topic representations into both visual and textual encoders, a Context-Gated Calibration (CGC) module that modulates feature magnitudes based on contextual consistency, and a Projected Bilinear Block Fusion (PBBF) head capturing low-rank multiplicative cross-modal interactions.

Experiments on CrisisMMD and TSEqD show macro-F1 gains of +4.35 and +2.56 points over strong multimodal baselines, and CAFuNet surpasses zero-shot Gemini 2.0 Flash by over +28.75 F1 points.

🗂️
Module 01

Dual-Topic Conditioning

Topics induced via BERTopic, curated by NPMI coherence. Learnable embeddings prepended to both text and visual patch sequences.

🎛️
Module 02

Context-Gated Calibration

Gaussian, Sigmoid, and Trapezoidal membership functions compute per-feature gating scores — amplifying reliable signals, suppressing noise.

🔀
Module 03

Projected Bilinear Fusion

B parallel blocks project modalities into lower-dimension, compute element-wise products, concatenate for rich cross-modal representation.

02 IndicHate · EMNLP 2025

Shahid Shafi Dar, Arjun Singh, Priya Sharma, Nagendra Kumar

Figure 1. IndicHate: A multilingual hate speech detection framework for Indian languages.

Abstract

Hate speech detection in Indian languages remains challenging due to code-mixing, limited annotated data, and cultural nuances. We introduce IndicHate, the first large-scale benchmark for hate speech detection covering 5 Indian languages: Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, and Marathi. Our dataset comprises 45,000 human-annotated social media posts collected from Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube comments.

We propose a multilingual transformer architecture with language-adaptive fine-tuning that leverages cross-lingual transfer from high-resource to low-resource Indian languages. Our model incorporates code-mixed tokenization and handles romanized script inputs commonly used in Indian social media.

IndicHate achieves 87.3% macro-F1 on Hindi, with competitive performance on low-resource languages like Marathi (74.2% F1). We also provide baselines using GPT-4 and Gemini, showing that specialized models outperform general-purpose LLMs by +18.6 F1 points on average.

🗣️
Component 1

Code-Mixed Tokenizer

Handles romanized Devanagari, Tamil, Telugu scripts with subword regularization for OOV words.

🔄
Component 2

Language-Adaptive Fine-Tuning

Continual pre-training on 100M Indian social media tokens before task-specific fine-tuning.

⚖️
Component 3

Culture-Aware Detection

Contextual embeddings with cultural entity recognition for caste, religion, and regional references.

03 IndicCrisis · ICWSM 2026

Shahid Shafi Dar, Mihir Kanchan Karandikar, Pranjal Pandey, Rohan Gupta, Nagendra Kumar

Figure 1. IndicCrisis: First multilingual multimodal crisis dataset for India covering 6 disaster types.

Abstract

India faces frequent humanitarian crises including floods, cyclones, earthquakes, landslides, and civil unrest. However, existing crisis informatics datasets focus primarily on English content from Western disasters, leaving a critical gap for multilingual Indian scenarios. We present IndicCrisis, the first large-scale multilingual multimodal crisis dataset covering 6 disaster types across 4 Indian languages (Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, English) with 32,000 text-image pairs.

We propose a multilingual crisis-specific adapter that extends CAFuNet to handle multiple Indian languages simultaneously through language-agnostic topic conditioning. Our model achieves 84.6% macro-F1 on disaster classification, outperforming multilingual CLIP and Gemini 2.0 by +12.3% and +21.7% respectively.

We release the dataset, trained models, and benchmarks to accelerate crisis response research in the Indian subcontinent.

🇮🇳
Contribution 1

Multilingual Crisis Dataset

32K text-image pairs, 6 disaster types, 4 languages with human verification.

🌐
Contribution 2

Language-Agnostic Adapter

Cross-lingual transfer using mT5 + Vision Transformer with language-agnostic topic conditioning.

🚨
Contribution 3

Real-Time Deployment

Optimized for low-latency inference (~45ms) for emergency response applications.
